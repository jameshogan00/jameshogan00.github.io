Knockbridge Tidy Town

About

Knockbridge is a small village within the townland of Ballinlough (Baile an Locha) in County Louth, Ireland. The village takes its name from "Cnoic Bhríde" - Bridget's Hill - reputed to be a site connected with local saint Bridget. Nearby is Cuchulainn's Stone, which still stands, and where the legendary hero is reputed to have died.

The village is centred on a crossroads with its most famous being Stephenstown House, a large ruined Victorian house, once owned by a branch of the Fortescue family. The house stands beside the River Fane, about a mile outside the village.

Stephenstown Pond, about a hundred metres from the house, was redeveloped in the mid-1990s and is a public amenity. Stephenstown Pond has a conference centre and an 8,000 sq ft (740 m2) community enterprise space. It is a habitat for a large number of animals.

Tidy Town Participation

The community of Knockbridge has always taken pride in the maintenance and appearence of their town. On the 1st of July 2008, such efforts were recognised when committee members were presented with the Best Kept Village award for the 32 counties of Ireland.

The people of Knockbridge have continuously strived to improve community connectivity and promote environmentally friendly activities amongst its inhabitants.
